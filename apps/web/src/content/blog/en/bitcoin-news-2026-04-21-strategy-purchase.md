## Introduction

In one of the most significant displays of corporate conviction in Bitcoin history, Strategy — the company formerly known as MicroStrategy — announced a purchase of 34,164 BTC for $2.54 billion last week. This was the company's highest single-week acquisition since November 2024, and the third-largest weekly purchase in its history.

The move sent a clear message to markets: institutional accumulation of Bitcoin is far from over. With this latest purchase, Strategy now holds 815,061 Bitcoin — equivalent to roughly 4% of Bitcoin's total supply of 21 million coins — at an aggregate cost of $61.56 billion.

## Why This Purchase Matters

### A Signal of Deep Conviction

Strategy's relentless accumulation strategy has made it one of the mostBitcoin-aligned companies in the world. Rather than treating Bitcoin as a reserve asset to be occasionally topped up, Strategy has embedded BTC purchases into its core identity — funding them through at-the-market (ATM) equity offerings and preferred stock sales.

This latest purchase demonstrates that the company sees the current price range as an opportunity to keep stacking sats, even at scale. With an aggregate cost basis now in the tens of billions, Strategy's leadership is clearly playing a long game anchored in Bitcoin's fixed supply and monetary properties.

### 4% of Total Supply

The figure that stands out most: 815,061 BTC represents approximately **4% of Bitcoin's entire supply**. To put that in perspective, no single entity — not even the largest governments or central banks — holds that percentage of gold reserves. Strategy has effectively become one of the most significant single holders of a global monetary asset in modern history.

This concentration matters because Bitcoin's supply is mathematically capped at 21 million coins. Every coin Strategy accumulates is one that cannot be mined again, one that leaves the circulating supply, and one that reinforces Bitcoin's narrative as the scarcest asset on Earth.

## The Broader Context: $1 Billion in Weekly ETF Inflows

Strategy's purchase didn't happen in isolation. It came amid the strongest week for Bitcoin exchange-traded products (ETPs) since January, with total inflows reaching approximately $1 billion — representing roughly 71% of the $1.4 billion that flowed into all crypto investment products last week.

According to CoinShares' weekly digital asset report, this surge in demand was supported by:

* **Easing geopolitical tensions** — including the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz by Iran, which temporarily pushed Bitcoin past $76,000
* **Favorable US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data**, which reduced pressure on interest rate expectations
* **Renewed institutional confidence** in Bitcoin as a macro hedge and store of value

These inflows signal that large players — from asset managers to sovereign entities — are re-entering the market after a period of consolidation.

## Bitcoin Holds $75K Amid Geopolitical Pressure

Bitcoin is currently trading around $75,800, up roughly 0.9% over the past 24 hours. The market's ability to hold above $75,000 — even amid geopolitical friction involving the US and Iran in the Strait of Hormuz — reflects a maturing asset class that is increasingly decoupling from traditional risk-on/risk-off dynamics.

For Bitcoin holders, this resilience is meaningful. Historically, geopolitical tensions have triggered capital flight into safe-haven assets. Bitcoin's ability to absorb shocks like US-Iran maritime clashes without collapsing demonstrates its growing status as a legitimate alternative reserve asset, not just a speculative trade.

## The STRC Dividend Change: What Investors Should Know

Strategy also revealed a notable administrative change: the dividend payment schedule for its STRC (Variable Rate Series A Perpetual Stretch Preferred Stock) will shift from **monthly to semi-monthly**, pending shareholder approval at the company's annual meeting in June.

If approved, the new schedule — with the first semi-monthly payment on July 15 — is designed to:

* Reduce reinvestment lag for preferred stockholders
* Improve liquidity and market efficiency
* Support greater price stability for STRC holders

This change reflects Strategy's ongoing effort to make its equity products more attractive to institutional capital, which in turn funds more Bitcoin purchases — creating a self-reinforcing cycle of accumulation.

## Conclusion

Strategy's $2.54 billion Bitcoin purchase is more than a headline. It is a data point in a larger story of institutional adoption, monetary hardening, and the ongoing transfer of value from fiat-denominated balance sheets to Bitcoin's decentralized ledger.

With 4% of Bitcoin's total supply now held by a single publicly traded company, and with ETF inflows hitting their highest levels since January, the signals point in one direction: the institutional phase of Bitcoin adoption is accelerating, not slowing.

For educational Bitcoin investors, this is a reminder of why the fundamentals matter. Scarcity, decentralization, and growing institutional validation continue to build the case for Bitcoin as a foundational layer of a new monetary system.
